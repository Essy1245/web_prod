Interests

3D Printing

I love being able to manufacture (almost) whatever I want. It gives me so much freedom in the ways I think and approach problems. I also learned so much about robotics, mechanics, and mechanical principles from fixing 3D printers.

VR Gaming

I can have fun, AND exercise? Sign me up!

I mostly play Recroom (Quest for the Golden Trophy, The Rise of Jumbotrion, Paintball, etc) and Beat Saber.

Material You (Design)

I really love Google's Material You design. It creates a clean, unified look across many applications and devices and is the perfect example of "Simple is better," but not taking it too far. This website partially reflects the clean, round, and flat design principles that Google loves.

I also love Google's own Google Sans font, but unfortunately, it's strictly copyrighted :(

Glassmorphism (Design)

Glassmorphism is a trending design technique where buttons or backgrounds look similar to glass. I love the look of frosted glass, and it can help make fancy 3D backgrounds appear flat.

Also helps user focus on the content with the fancy background.

My social links have example! Find "Become Friends" from the navigation menu.

Drones

What the hell, it flies?

I have a couple drones myself. From super cinematic camera ones, to tiny indoor racing ones. Why? I just like flying.

I get to experience familiar enviroments as if I was a bee (a really fat one). I just like flying through, over, and under things. Fresh perspective!

#GetTheMessage

Google has been trying hard to make Apple #GetTheMessage. Basically, iPhones under iOS18 used outdated texting protocol that only worked well only between iPhones through proprietary software, AKA iMessage. This results in broken group chats, blurry media when texting between iPhone and non-iPhones, and broken reactions. Have you ever received a text saying "[Person] Liked your message"? That's what Google's been trying to fix.

Luckily, RCS was finally brought to iPhones with the release of iOS18. I can finally make a group chat with peace.

This is one of the things that I think was just dumb. This issue was 100% artificially created and was totally preventable. Imagine trying to explain this to an alien.

Android

I heavily believe in having control over what I own. In my opinion, Androids are far superior to iPhones because of the flexibility we get. App not on Google Play store? Sideload it! Don't like an app? Modify it!

I hate the peeps that go 'oOH wHaT aBouT seCuriTY?"

I want you to think about when was the last time you heard about a phone getting hacked.

Android still has its own security features.

Regardless, I think that the device's security depends heavily on the user. So for someone like elderly people where they can't tell between a malicious advertisement and the download button, sure, iOS might be better for them. But if you know what you're doing, Android offers far more flexibility and options.

Plus, my Android folds. Your iPhone can do that too? Well, mine can unfold too.

Things that move

Even as a child, I was never fascinated by Legos or other static toys. I was always interested in things that moved. Things that did something.

Those things also happen to be more mechanically complex and sophisticated.

Therefore, I liked drones, RC cars, and RC airplanes. Maybe that's why I'm pursuing my career in aerospace engineering.

Metric

I love metric. Metric all the way!!!

Un-interests (I hate these)

Writing

I used to belive I hate writing because my English was not sufficient. Recently, I discovered that is not the case. I scored higher on the English section of SAT than quite a few of my American friends.

I especially hate it when teachers give out super-long writing assignments. I can NEVER reach the word count just by yapping all I want. I end up bringing unrelated details to meet the word count, then get points off for bringing those in.

Why don't teachers ask, "How short can you write?"

It'll take less time for us to write, so less hate on the assignment and the teacher. Teachers can grade it faster. They can ultimately test how efficient we can be with each sentence, which is what I think that matters.

Tesla

I hate Tesla for a few reasons, especially the new ones. Enviroment is not a big aspect of it.
I thought they looked cool inside and out, and they do. They did an excellent job with the clean interior. However, the fact that I have to scroll up in the center screen to adjust where the AC points is just straight up unacceptable. Having everything at the center screen looks clean, but voice assistants are far from perfect. We still need physical control. From manufacturing standpoint, yes, that is easier, but this comes at the cost of consumers' safety.

There are few automakers who solved this problem "correctly".

Look at Polstar 3. Everything is on the screen, but you get physical HVAC control even with the clean interior.

Look at Xiaomi SU7. You can add the buttons if you like, which is even better.

Another thing about Tesla is that their service is terrible. They grew too quickly while the support/repair infrastructure wasn't catching up well.

EV, in general, exposes everyone to a good amount electromagnetic radiation. There are studies going on about it, and they have mixed answers. Do note that this same type of radiation was classified to be a possible carcinogenic to human (2B classification). Don't drive in your microwave, especially daily.
